Hey Product Hunt 👋

I built Yap because I realized I don't respond to productivity apps. I respond to guilt.

Every to-do app I've tried did the same thing: set a task, get a polite reminder, ignore it, feel nothing. The tree in Forest died. My Todoist has 23 overdue tasks. The Pomodoro timer is still "paused" from last Tuesday.

What actually makes me do things: my mom calling. The feeling that someone noticed I haven't started. Social pressure from someone who has opinions about my life choices.

So I built that into an app.

Yap has 12 AI agents. You pick one, set a task, set a deadline. They send push notifications that escalate the longer you wait. Every message is generated fresh by GPT-4o, never the same twice.

The agents:
🫵 Mom — guilt that escalates to book-club-level gossip
💔 The Ex — "typical" (somehow the most effective word in the English language)
🪖 Drill Sergeant — ALL CAPS. NO MERCY. YOUR LAUNDRY IS A WARZONE.
👵 Grandma — survived a war, can't believe you can't clean a kitchen
🛋️ Therapist — "what's really stopping you?" ...then waits
🛸 Conspiracy Theorist — "they don't WANT you to finish that essay"
👔 Boss — corporate passive-aggression in notification form
🫶 Best Friend — hypes you up, then roasts you when you slack
😊 The Colleague — "no no, go ahead, I'LL handle it. Again."
...and 3 more unhinged options

The thing I'm most proud of: the leaderboard doesn't rank users. It ranks agents. When you fail, it's not "you gave up" — it's "your agent didn't deliver." Removes the shame, keeps the motivation.

Some agents remember your past missions. They keep receipts. If you gave up on "morning jog" last week, The Ex will mention it. Zero mercy.

Free: 6 agents + 1 mission/day. Pro ($9.99 once): all 12, unlimited missions, agent memory.

iOS only for now. Built with SwiftUI + Supabase + GPT-4o.

Happy to answer any questions. And if you want a promo code — tell me which agent you'd pick. If you say "The Ex" I will judge you silently.

POST 1 - "Mom got a promotion"
────────────────────────────────────────

I put my mom in an app and 10,000 people downloaded her.

Now she has 11 colleagues.

Last year I shipped hey - a friendship reminder app. You add your people, the app nudges you when you haven't reached out. The killer feature wasn't the tracking. It was the notification tone. Users could choose Sarcastic, Passive-Aggressive, Motivational, or Boomer Mom.

Boomer Mom won. By a lot.

"HELLO!!! Have you called Tom yet??? Love, Mom"

That taught me something: people don't respond to information. They respond to personality. "You haven't texted Sarah in 14 days" gets swiped. "Not to be dramatic, but Sarah might forget what you look like soon" gets a text sent.

So I took that insight and built Yap.

12 AI agents. Productivity app. You set a task, pick a deadline, choose who nags you. The messages are AI-generated, never repeat, and escalate the longer you wait. You can share your weaknesses - the agents use them against you.

Mom still does guilt. But now she has:
- The Ex ("typical")
- Drill Sergeant (ALL CAPS)
- Grandma (survived a war, you can't finish one task)
- The Therapist ("what's really stopping you?" ...then waits)

In hey, Mom sent you one nudge.
In Yap, Mom sends you 6 notifications that get progressively more unhinged until you clean the apartment.

She got promoted. She earned it.

6 agents are free. Mom included. She's enough for most people.

POST 2 - "The lock screen is the product"
────────────────────────────────────────

Every productivity app requires you to open it.

That's already the problem. If you had the discipline to open a productivity app at 3pm on a Sunday, you probably don't need one.

I built an app that comes to you.

Yap has 12 AI agents - Mom, The Ex, Drill Sergeant, Grandma, and 8 more. You set a task and a deadline. The agent sends push notifications that escalate the longer you wait. Every message is AI-generated, never repeats, and gets worse over time.

The lock screen does all the work. You don't have to open the app. You just have to feel the pressure.

There's also a leaderboard - but it doesn't rank users. It ranks agents. When you fail, it's not "you gave up." It's "your agent didn't deliver."

Turns out removing shame and replacing it with a scoreboard works.

6 agents free. Pro unlocks all 12.

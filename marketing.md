────────────────────────────────────────
POST 1 - "Mom got a promotion"
────────────────────────────────────────

I put my mom in an app and 10,000 people downloaded her.

Now she has 11 colleagues.

Last year I shipped hey - a friendship reminder app. You add your people, the app nudges you when you haven't reached out. The killer feature wasn't the tracking. It was the notification tone. Users could choose Sarcastic, Passive-Aggressive, Motivational, or Boomer Mom.

Boomer Mom won. By a lot.

"HELLO!!! Have you called Tom yet??? Love, Mom"

That taught me something: people don't respond to information. They respond to personality. "You haven't texted Sarah in 14 days" gets swiped. "Not to be dramatic, but Sarah might forget what you look like soon" gets a text sent.

So I took that insight and built Yap.

12 AI agents. Productivity app. You set a task, pick a deadline, choose who nags you. The messages are AI-generated, never repeat, and escalate the longer you wait. You can share your weaknesses - the agents use them against you.

Mom still does guilt. But now she has:
- The Ex ("typical")
- Drill Sergeant (ALL CAPS)
- Grandma (survived a war, you can't finish one task)
- The Therapist ("what's really stopping you?" ...then waits)

In hey, Mom sent you one nudge.
In Yap, Mom sends you 6 notifications that get progressively more unhinged until you clean the apartment.

She got promoted. She earned it.

6 agents are free. Mom included. She's enough for most people.
